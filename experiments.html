Hydraulic Press Experiments

Hydraulic presses demonstrate force and pressure. Below are descriptions of common experiments (for safety, use models or professionals; do not attempt dangerous crushing at home).

Model Hydraulic Lift

Use syringes as cylinders connected by tubing filled with water. Push a small syringe to lift a load on a larger one, showing force multiplication.

Materials: Two syringes (different sizes), tubing, water. Connect and test lifting small weights.

Crushing Paper Stack

Press 1000 sheets of paper to observe compression and deformation.

Demonstrates material limits under high pressure (e.g., 100 tons).

Crushing Everyday Objects

Test items like books or foam balls for satisfying deformation.

Shows how pressure affects different materials; often used in videos for education.

Material Strength Test

Press metals or welds to measure breaking points.

Useful in labs; e.g., testing acrylic joints.
