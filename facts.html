Key Facts About Hydraulic Presses

  • Invented by Joseph Bramah in 1795, also known as the Bramah press.
  • Operates on Pascal's principle: Pressure in a closed fluid system is constant and transmitted equally.
  • Components include a reservoir for fluid, pump, valves, and cylinders (ram and plunger).
  • Should run cool (120-140Â°F max) with no leaks for optimal performance.
  • Simple design, easy to operate, and lasts years with proper maintenance.
  • Versatile designs: H-frame, C-frame, horizontal, and more.
  • Uses incompressible fluid (usually oil) to multiply force.
  • Overheating causes 25% of issues; regular checks prevent this.
  • Powered by manual, pneumatic, or electric pumps.
  • Economical for intricate shapes and material efficiency.
